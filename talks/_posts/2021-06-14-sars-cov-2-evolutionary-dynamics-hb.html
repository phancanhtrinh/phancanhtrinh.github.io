Evolutionary rate in context

Up to December, my expectation was evolution as seen in seasonal coronaviruses

Kistler and Bedford. 2021. eLife.

OC43 and 229E show flu B-like rates of adaptive evolution in S1

Kistler and Bedford. 2021. eLife.

SARS-CoV-2 evolves at a rate of ~3.4 subs per year in spike S1

Spike S1 rate of ~3.4 subs per year similar to HA1 of flu A

Three scenarios for ongoing viral evolution

  1. Seasonal coronavirus-like scenario: SARS-CoV-2 evolution slows after selection for replication completes. S1 is relatively inflexible in terms of antigenic evolution. New strains emerge rarely and replace existing diversity. Vaccine updates required every ~5 years.
  2. Influenza H3N2-like scenario: SARS-CoV-2 evolution slows after selection for replication completes, but S1 remains highly antigenically flexible. New strains emerge often and replace existing diversity. Vaccine updates required every 1-2 years.
  3. HIV-like scenario: SARS-CoV-2 evolution slows after selection for replication completes, but S1 remains highly antigenically flexible. New strains emerge and co-circulate leading to continual antigenic diversification. Vaccine updates cannot cover diversity of circulating viruses.
